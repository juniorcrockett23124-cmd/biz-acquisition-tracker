# Research Memo - Established Managed IT Services Provider with Recurring Revenue

## Core Thesis

The underlying business model is attractive, but the current valuation looks aggressive relative to disclosed cash flow, so this is only compelling if contract quality and growth upside are stronger than the listing suggests.

## Market And Industry Notes

Managed IT is one of the better recurring-revenue service categories because contracts can be sticky and upsellable. The weakest MSP acquisitions are owner-led sales organizations with shallow process and high customer concentration.

## What Is Attractive

- Recurring services explicitly called out.
- Existing contractor/operator layer.
- Category has strong holdco-style appeal if churn is low.

## What Is Dangerous

- Price looks full.
- Contractor reliance can weaken transferability.
- Revenue mix may include too much project work.

## Must-Ask Diligence Questions

- What percentage of revenue is MRR/contracted?
- What is gross margin by managed services versus project work?
- Are the contractors exclusive and durable?
- What is churn for the top 20 accounts?
