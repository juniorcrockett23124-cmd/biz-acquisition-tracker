# Research Memo - $700,000+ Revenue CPA Firm In Union County

## Core Thesis

This is one of the more attractive current accounting targets because the listing pairs strong cash flow with a paperless workflow and unusually explicit transition help, but it still needs confirmation that staff and client relationships are durable enough to survive the sale announcement.

## Market And Industry Notes

The broader accounting market remains solid for firms that provide recurring compliance and advisory support rather than one-time tax preparation alone. BLS projects 5% employment growth for accountants and auditors from 2024 to 2034, and AICPA continues to identify staffing and succession as leading small-firm issues. That creates two simultaneous realities: buyer demand for decent firms remains strong, and seller supply from retirement-driven exits should persist. A paperless, portal-based practice is better positioned than a paper-heavy legacy shop, especially in a dense New Jersey market where clients increasingly expect digital workflows and fast turnaround.

## What Is Attractive

- Cash flow of roughly $449K is strong relative to the asking price and fits the buy box well.
- Paperless operations and portal-based workflows should improve transferability and reduce operational friction.
- The seller plus a senior CPA are both willing to help on a per-diem basis, which is unusually useful transition support.
- The service mix appears broad enough to support recurring client touchpoints beyond April tax deadlines.
- Retirement motivation looks credible rather than vague.

## What Is Dangerous

- Staff are not yet aware of the sale, so the transition plan could look cleaner on paper than in reality.
- If one senior CPA or the owner carries most review authority, client retention could dip after close.
- Union County and the surrounding North Jersey market are competitive, so weak service quality can cause clients to leave quietly over time.
- High cash flow margins can sometimes reflect deferred staffing investment or an overloaded owner rather than a truly turnkey model.
- Without clean client concentration data, a few family groups or business owners could drive too much revenue.

## Must-Ask Diligence Questions

- What is the exact revenue mix across tax, bookkeeping, payroll, compilations, and advisory work?
- Who owns the top 20 client relationships today, and how portable are those relationships away from the seller?
- What is the tenure, compensation, and retention risk of the senior CPA and other staff members?
- How many clients are businesses versus individuals, and what percent of revenue is recurring before tax season begins?
- Has the firm experienced meaningful client churn after prior staff changes or partner absences?
- Can the seller document a transition schedule through at least one filing cycle, and will any seller carry backstop retention?

- Recommendation: buy / pursue
