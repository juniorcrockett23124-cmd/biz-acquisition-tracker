# Research Memo - $600,000 Eastern Bucks County PA Tax Accounting Firm

## Core Thesis

This is a strong candidate if the payroll and accounting work create a real recurring base supported by staff and documented systems, but it is still one diligence layer away from the top tier because the listing does not yet prove team depth or owner independence.

## Market And Industry Notes

Accounting practices remain attractive acquisition targets where recurring service lines offset tax-season cyclicality. BLS projects 5% growth in accountant and auditor employment from 2024 to 2034, while bookkeeping clerk roles are projected to decline 6%, which favors firms that move beyond commodity data entry into higher-trust compliance and business support. AICPA continues to highlight staffing and succession as persistent small-firm issues, so retirement-driven exits should remain available, but buyers still need to confirm whether they are purchasing a team-based platform or simply an aging owner's personal book. A paperless, portal-based firm in Bucks County should compare well against older manual shops if the process claims are real.

## What Is Attractive

- Cash flow of about $370K sits squarely in the target range.
- Tax, accounting, and payroll together imply better stickiness than tax prep alone.
- Paperless and portal-based workflows are strong transferability signals.
- Retirement motivation appears explicit rather than inferred.
- The asking price is still workable for SBA-style financing if the recurring component is validated.

## What Is Dangerous

- Team depth is still mostly unknown, which is the biggest reason this is not yet in the top bucket.
- Payroll can be sticky, but it can also be operationally fragile if only one person knows the process.
- If the practice is more tax-heavy than the description suggests, cash flow quality could be overstated.
- Portal usage does not automatically mean clean SOPs, role redundancy, or modern controls.
- Client concentration and seller relationship intensity remain unproven.

## Must-Ask Diligence Questions

- How much revenue is monthly payroll/accounting versus annual tax work?
- How many staff members are there, what are their roles, and who can independently run payroll and client communications?
- What is net client retention, and how many clients account for the first 25%, 50%, and 75% of revenue?
- Which clients interact directly with staff today versus only with the owner?
- What software stack and documented workflows support tax, payroll, and bookkeeping delivery?
- What seller transition support and financing flexibility are available?
