# Research Memo - Growing IT & Web Hosting MSP w/ Recurring Revs $300K in SDE

## Core Thesis

This is a credible cash-flowing MSP with real tenure and recurring revenue, but the most important question is whether the durable asset is contracted client relationships or simply a mature owner-operated technical practice with a small staff.

## Market And Industry Notes

The MSP market is still growing, but 2026 conditions are harder than a few years ago. Kaseya's 2026 MSP report says nearly half of MSPs rank AI and automation as the top client need, customer acquisition is the top challenge, larger deal sizes have compressed sharply, and talent shortages are worsening. That cuts both ways here: demand for outsourced IT remains durable, especially in security and continuity work, but small generalist MSPs can get squeezed if they lack specialized offerings or depend on legacy hosting revenue. The listing's Microsoft and Dell partner credentials help, but the business still needs proof it can defend margins as buyers ask for more cybersecurity, compliance, and AI-related capability.

## What Is Attractive

- Thirty-six years in market is unusually strong for a sub-$1M revenue MSP.
- The client mix across SMBs and nonprofits in multiple Southeastern states suggests some diversification.
- Three employees is lean but at least indicates the seller is not literally the only technician.
- Recurring managed services and hosting revenue should finance better than project-heavy IT work if churn is low.
- Price to SDE is still workable for SBA-style financing if customer retention is solid.

## What Is Dangerous

- The listing is marked sale pending, which may reflect demand but also means timing and access could be poor.
- Web hosting can be sticky, but it can also hide legacy infrastructure, thin margins, and support obligations that are hard to unwind.
- A three-person shop may still have key-man risk concentrated in the owner or one senior engineer.
- Industry pressure is moving toward cybersecurity, compliance, automation, and AI-enabled services; a plain-vanilla MSP can drift into commodity pricing.
- Multi-state support raises questions about onsite obligations, travel, and how much work is truly remote.

## Must-Ask Diligence Questions

- What is monthly recurring revenue by line: MSP, cloud, hosting, backup, security, and project work?
- What share of clients buy cybersecurity or BCDR services, and what is attach rate per customer?
- How many tickets, escalations, and client QBRs still route through the owner?
- Is hosting built on owned infrastructure, third-party cloud, or reseller arrangements?
- What was logo churn and net revenue retention in 2024 and 2025?
- Are there any oversized nonprofit, healthcare, or regulated clients that require specialized compliance capability?
