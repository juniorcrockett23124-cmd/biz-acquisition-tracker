# Research Memo - Established Managed Service Provider (MSP)-Cybersecurity-Led Delivery

## Core Thesis

This is a high-quality target if the recurring cybersecurity work is truly contract-backed and delivered by a team that can retain clients without the owner, but the premium valuation means even modest customer concentration or owner-centric technical authority would materially weaken the deal.

## Market And Industry Notes

The market backdrop is favorable for well-run MSPs because cybersecurity complexity keeps rising for small and midsize customers that cannot staff everything internally. BLS projects computer and information technology occupations overall to grow faster than average from 2024 to 2034, and computer and information systems managers to grow 15% over the same period, which supports continued outsourced demand for security and infrastructure help. The industry risk is that cybersecurity-led MSPs also carry concentrated operational and reputational downside: CISA has repeatedly warned that MSP compromise can cascade across many downstream customers at once. For a buyer, that makes process maturity, documentation, privileged-access control, and incident-response discipline far more important than the headline SDE alone.

## What Is Attractive

- The cybersecurity positioning should create stronger retention and better pricing than commodity help-desk or break-fix work.
- Approximately 20 active clients is a manageable base if concentration is not excessive and contract economics are healthy.
- Three full-time employees is enough to suggest a real operating core rather than a pure solo practice.
- Retirement motivation and likely seller support improve transition odds.
- At roughly $515K of cash flow, the business can carry debt better than many subscale MSP listings.

## What Is Dangerous

- At a $2.1M ask, the valuation already assumes low churn, strong documentation, and limited owner dependence.
- A small cybersecurity shop can be extremely key-person dependent if the owner still owns sales engineering, escalation support, vendor relationships, or incident command.
- One serious security failure during or after transition could damage renewals quickly.
- Client concentration can hide inside a seemingly diversified 20-account base if a few customers represent most MRR.
- Tool sprawl, weak password hygiene, poor backup practices, or nonstandard onboarding can turn a good-looking MSP into a post-close cleanup project.

## Must-Ask Diligence Questions

- What percent of revenue is contracted managed services, cybersecurity monitoring, project work, and hardware resale?
- How many clients are on annual agreements, what is net revenue retention, and what was logo churn in 2024 and 2025?
- Which technical functions still require the owner personally: sales engineering, vCISO work, escalation support, vendor management, or compliance reviews?
- What certifications, tenure, and customer-facing responsibilities sit with each of the three employees?
- What RMM, PSA, backup, endpoint, SIEM, documentation, and privileged-access tools are in use, and are they standardized?
- Has the business had any material security incidents, insurance claims, or customer terminations tied to service quality or cyber events?
- Is seller carry available to offset valuation and align retention outcomes?
