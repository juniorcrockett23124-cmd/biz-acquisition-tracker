# Research Memo - Virtual Bookkeeping And Accounting Firm

## Core Thesis

This is one of the cleaner current fits because the delivery model is remote, the revenue is described as fully recurring, and the owner already appears out of daily production, but the asking multiple leaves little room for weak retention or staff turnover.

## Market And Industry Notes

The market backdrop is favorable for outsourced finance support but uneven for commodity bookkeeping. BLS projects accountants and auditors to grow 5% from 2024 to 2034, while bookkeeping clerk employment is projected to decline 6%, which reinforces the split between higher-value advisory/compliance work and automatable transaction processing. For small firms, AICPA continues to identify staffing, succession, and AI integration as leading issues. That means a virtual accounting platform can be valuable if it combines recurring workflows, disciplined systems, and stable people, but weaker shops can get compressed by software automation and offshore competition.

## What Is Attractive

- Fifteen years of operating history and a nationwide client base give this more credibility than a newly assembled remote agency.
- A seasoned four-person team and owner-light model are exactly what the buy box wants.
- Auto-renewing contracts imply better continuity than month-to-month informal bookkeeping relationships.
- Fully remote delivery expands labor flexibility and reduces fixed overhead.
- The listing is already under offer, which suggests market validation of the core thesis.

## What Is Dangerous

- At roughly 4.1x SDE, valuation is full for a small service business unless churn is exceptionally low.
- "Nationwide client base" can also mean diffuse service standards, fragmented state-tax exposure, and harder client intimacy.
- If the service mix skews too far toward basic bookkeeping, automation and lower-cost competitors can pressure pricing.
- Key staff loss would be especially painful in a remote setting where process discipline must substitute for founder oversight.
- The listing does not clarify whether tax, controller, or advisory work is included, which matters a lot for durability.

## Must-Ask Diligence Questions

- What percent of revenue is bookkeeping versus controller, payroll, tax-adjacent, and advisory services?
- Are contracts annual with auto-renewal, or cancellable on short notice?
- What was gross and net client retention for 2024 and 2025?
- Are any team members offshore, contract-based, or otherwise at elevated retention risk?
- What systems run the operation today, and are SOPs strong enough for a clean handoff?
- Is there seller carry or any earnout tied to retention at this valuation?
