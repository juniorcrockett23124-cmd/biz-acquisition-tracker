# Research Memo - Consulting, Bookkeeping and Accounting Firm

## Core Thesis

The recurring bookkeeping angle is attractive, but this deal hinges on whether delivery is team-based and process-driven rather than owner-driven.

## Market And Industry Notes

Bookkeeping and outsourced accounting remain strong search-fund style categories because they are sticky, serviceable remotely, and can often be professionalized. Small firms with weak documentation can still be fragile.

## What Is Attractive

- Recurring accounting/bookkeeping work.
- Cash flow fits the buy box.
- Lower purchase price than some competing listings.

## What Is Dangerous

- Team depth is unclear.
- Advisory or consulting revenue may be less repeatable than monthly bookkeeping.
- Customer concentration could be hidden inside a small client base.

## Must-Ask Diligence Questions

- How much revenue is monthly retainer versus project work?
- Who currently services the accounts besides the owner?
- What does onboarding and month-end close workflow look like?
- Are there offshore or contractor dependencies?

## Recommendation

- Recommendation: watch / pursue if team is real
