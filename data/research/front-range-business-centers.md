# Research Memo - Front Range Business Centers

## Core Thesis

Interesting recurring-membership angle, but not as clean a fit as accounting or MSP targets.

## Industry / Market Notes

- Category: Office / Virtual Office
- Broad view: recurring business services, payroll, bookkeeping, MSP, and property management businesses generally underwrite better than one-off project shops because retention and transferability can be stronger.
- Key question: does this company actually behave like a system-driven business or is the owner still the product?

## What Is Attractive

- 18 years in business suggests durability.
- Reported cash flow of $200K is inside the target buy box.
- Public positioning suggests moderate recurring revenue characteristics.

## What Is Dangerous

- Recurring revenue may be real, but occupancy/member churn and operational leverage need proof.
- Retirement motivation remains unverified publicly.

## Must-Ask Diligence Questions

- Is the owner actively retiring, and what is the exact timeline?
- How many full-time employees are in place, and who runs day-to-day operations?
- What percentage of revenue is contract or recurring versus one-off work?
- What are the top five customers and their share of revenue?
- How much of the current cash flow depends on the owner personally?
- Is seller financing available and on what terms?
